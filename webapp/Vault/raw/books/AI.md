# Artificial Intelligence

Simulating or Creating Intelligence has many sub-problems

*   **Knowledge representation** - representation of what exists (objects, relationships, properties)
*   **Learning** - study of algorithms that improve automatically through experience
*   **Natural language processing** - read and understand human language
*   **Perception** - use input from sensors (cameras, radar, mics, sonar) to deduce aspects of the world
*   **Social Intelligence** - process, interpret and simulate human emotion, mood
*   **General Intelligence** - broad mimicking of human intelligence

# Different Classes of Problems for AI

*   Descriptive – What is happening?
*   Diagnostic – Why did this happen?
*   Predictive – What might happen in future?
*   Prescriptive – What should we do? How should we do it?
*   Generative – Create something new

[Page 22]
# Descriptive Questions

*   AI analyses data to identify patterns and trends.
*   Answers the question, "What is the current state of things?"
*   Examples –
    *   Sales Reports: Showing performance by region, product, etc.
    *   Fraud Detection: Identifying unusual transactions.

[Page 23]
# Diagnostic Questions
* Goes deeper than just recognizing patterns.
* AI helps figure out the root cause of an event or situation.
* Examples –
    * Medical Diagnosis: Analysing tests and symptoms to find diseases.
    * Machine Breakdowns: Identifying reasons for equipment malfunctions.

[Page 24]
# Predictive Questions

*   Uses historical data to forecast likely outcomes.
*   Answers the question, "What are the chances of something happening?"
*   Examples –
    *   Demand Forecasting: Predicting future product sales.
    *   Customer Churn: Identifying which customers are likely to leave.

[Page 25]
# Prescriptive Questions
* AI recommends the best course of action.
* Helps to optimize decisions and outcomes.
* Examples –
    * Inventory Management: Determining the right stock levels.
    * Personalized Marketing: Suggesting offers for individual customers.

[Page 26]
# Generative Questions
*   AI produces original content such as text, images, code, and more.
*   Expands possibilities by 'imagining' solutions and content.
*   Examples –
    *   AI Art: Generating unique and creative images.
    *   Copywriting: Producing website copy or product descriptions.

# More Examples

*   Customer Churn Prediction – Identify customers who are likely to churn
    *   Past behaviour + data of - if they churned or not
    *   Models learns the patterns that distinguish customers who churned from those who didn't
    *   Business benefit: Build proactive retention strategies for high-risk customers
*   Sales Forecasting – Predict future sales growth
    *   Historical sales data, economic indicators, marketing campaigns, seasonality
    *   Models learns relationship between factors and sales outcomes
    *   Business benefit: Improves sales planning, production / inventory planning

# Rec Systems

    *   User rating, purchase history, browsing data

    *   Recommends what is possibly valuable

# History as Defamiliarization Engine: How Temporal Analogy Powers Digital Strategy Pedagogy

## Core Insight

Deepa Mani's use of historical analogy in teaching digital strategy is not merely illustrative decoration — it functions as a **defamiliarization engine**: a deliberate epistemic tool designed to reorient executives by making the familiar strange and the strange familiar. The mechanism is precise and reproducible.

## The Four-Stage Pedagogical Scaffold

Mani consistently deploys historical analogy within a macro-to-micro pedagogical arc:

1. **Macro forces** — Establish the long arc of general-purpose technology diffusion (electricity → IT → AI/IoT)
2. **Dramatic case** — Anchor the arc in a vivid, concrete historical moment (factory reorganization with electricity)
3. **Framework** — Introduce the structural tool (Digital Matrix, Business Ecosystem, etc.)
4. **Application** — Return the audience to their own industry, now re-perceived

History occupies Stage 1 and 2. Its function is to *pre-weaken* the audience's ideological resistance before the framework arrives in Stage 3. By the time a structural argument is made, executives have already conceded the pattern on neutral historical ground.

## Why the Electricity-to-AI Analogy Works

The electricity analogy is not interchangeable with any other historical parallel. It works for three specific reasons:

### 1. It Collapses Industry Uniqueness
Every executive believes their industry is categorically different. The electricity arc — which crossed manufacturing, finance, retail, agriculture, and services without exception — demonstrates that the *strategic-to-hygiene transition* is industry-agnostic. Resistance based on industry specificity is preemptively invalidated.

### 2. It Positions the Audience on a Known Trajectory
The arc has a shape executives can visualize: electricity was once strategic differentiation; it became operational hygiene. IT has traversed the same arc. AI and IoT now occupy the 'strategic' position electricity once held. This means executives can locate themselves on the curve rather than confronting transformation as an undifferentiated threat.

### 3. It Weaponizes Domain Expertise Against Resistance
The same industry knowledge that generates resistance — "we've always done it this way," "our customers are different" — becomes the instrument of persuasion. Once an executive accepts the electricity parallel (which requires no digital expertise to evaluate), their own historical business knowledge confirms the prediction. Mani uses their competence to dissolve their competency trap.

## The Spatial + Temporal Analytical Lens

The deeper synthesis here connects two of Mani's major frameworks into a complementary pair:

| Dimension | Framework | What It Reveals |
|---|---|---|
| **Spatial / Structural** | Digital Business Ecosystem | Who participates, who orchestrates, how value flows |
| **Temporal / Dynamic** | Historical Technology Diffusion Arc | Where you sit on the diffusion curve; disruptor or casualty |

Neither framework is complete alone:
- The ecosystem view without the temporal arc produces a static map — useful for describing the present but blind to trajectory.
- The historical arc without the ecosystem view produces narrative momentum — compelling but structurally vague about *what to do*.

Together, they answer both the *where are we?* question (ecosystem position) and the *when are we?* question (diffusion stage). The combination is what Mani positions as a 'complete analytical lens.'

## The 'Neutrality' Gambit

A critical rhetorical move is Mani's deliberate framing of both lenses as analytically neutral — historical fact on one side, structural observation on the other. This bypasses the ideological resistance that explicitly normative arguments ("you must transform or die") reliably trigger in senior executives. The history is presented as merely descriptive; the ecosystem map as merely observational. The strategic urgency emerges as the audience's own conclusion, not the instructor's prescription.

This is a form of **Socratic scaffolding**: the instructor's role is to arrange the evidence such that the audience discovers the insight independently, which dramatically increases commitment to acting on it.

## Implications for Understanding Mani's Research-Teaching Integration

This synthesis reveals that Mani's pedagogical method is not separable from her research agenda. Her empirical work on technology diffusion, IT-enabled transformation, and ecosystem dynamics directly supplies the historical cases that power the defamiliarization engine. Research and pedagogy are not parallel tracks — research generates the raw material for reorientation, and pedagogy is the delivery mechanism.

This also explains why her [[persona/intellectual-evolution|intellectual evolution]] — rooted in watching the Indian IT industry transform in real time — produces a distinctive urgency in her teaching: she has *lived* a compressed version of the electricity arc and can speak to it with personal as well as scholarly authority.

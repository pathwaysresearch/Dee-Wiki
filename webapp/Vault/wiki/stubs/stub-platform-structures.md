# Platform Structures

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This source is a lecture presentation (FRNT – Session 6 by Vishal K) covering platform-mediated business networks as the dominant business model of the digital economy. It contrasts traditional pipeline/industrial economy models (supply-side economies of scale) with platform models (demand-side economies and network effects), examines the distinctive challenges and strategies for platform providers, and explores unique attributes of platform competition. Google's two-sided platform model — connecting users and advertisers through search, AdWords, and AdSense — is used as a primary case study.

## Key Claims

- The dominant business model of today is platform-mediated business networks, which differ fundamentally from traditional pipeline/industrial economy models
- Industrial economies rely on supply-side economies of scale (high fixed/marginal costs, price reduction through volume), while digital platform economies like Uber leverage low marginal costs, demand-side economies, and network effects
- Platforms are composed of components (hardware, software, services) and rules (standards, protocols, policies, contracts), and exhibit both cross-side and same-side network effects in two-sided networks
- Platform providers focus on ecosystem value rather than just consumer value, owning terms of trade and policies while serving both sides as revenue sources with low marginal costs
- Google's platform model attracts users with free world-class search (PageRank), Gmail, and other services ("eyeballs"), then monetizes through AdWords keyword advertising, AdSense display advertising, and Google Analytics on the advertiser side

Platform-mediated business networks, pipeline vs platform business models, supply-side vs demand-side economies of scale, network effects (cross-side and same-side), two-sided network structures, platform components and rules, Google's platform model, AdWords auction system, commercial intent keywords, platform competition strategies, ecosystem value vs consumer value, digital economy characteristics

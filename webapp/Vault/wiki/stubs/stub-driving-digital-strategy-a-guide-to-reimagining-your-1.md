# Driving Digital Strategy: A Guide to Reimagining Your Business

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

Written by Harvard Business School professor Sunil Gupta, this book provides a comprehensive framework for digital transformation organized around three pillars: reimagining your business, reevaluating your value chain, and reconnecting with your customers. Gupta argues that leaders who achieve transformative results go "all-in" on digital rather than treating digital strategy as separate from overall business strategy. Drawing on over a decade of research and work with scores of companies, the book addresses why common approaches—such as creating independent digital units, running isolated experiments, or solely pursuing cost efficiency through technology—often fail to produce transformative impact.

## Key Claims

- Leaders who achieve transformative digital results integrate digital strategy into their overall business strategy rather than treating it as a separate initiative
- Creating an independent digital unit (like Telefónica's London Digital group) often fails because it is "like launching a speedboat to turn around a large ship"—the speedboat takes off but does little to move the ship
- Relying solely on technology to reduce costs and improve operational efficiency implicitly assumes that technology will not fundamentally change your business
- Companies that lack digital DNA and startup agility can still take full advantage of digital-era opportunities if they adopt the right framework
- Resistance from existing organizational structures (e.g., country heads unconvinced by digital innovations) is a major barrier to successful digital transformation

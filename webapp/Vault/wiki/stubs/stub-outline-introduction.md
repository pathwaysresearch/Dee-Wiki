# Outline & Introduction

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This source is a lecture deck from "Frontier Tech – Session 8" by Vishal Karungulam, covering course outline, digital disruption theory, and strategic implications for incumbent companies. It draws on Clayton Christensen's disruption framework to explain how disruptive innovations emerge, why incumbents ignore them, and how disruptors move upmarket over time. The material includes a multi-module course outline spanning guest speakers, in-class case competitions, and topics like platform/product power, e-commerce, and the incumbent's response to tech-led disruption.

## Key Claims

- Every company wants to be a digital company; digital is central to strategy
- Disruption is a process, not a single fixed point, which is why incumbents tend to ignore it
- Disruptors often build business models fundamentally different from those of incumbents
- Incumbents chasing higher profits do not respond vigorously to disruptive threats
- Disruptors move upmarket, eventually delivering performance mainstream customers require, causing mainstream adoption in volume and "over-serving" the market

## Topics Covered

Digitization and what it means, disruption theory (Christensen framework), the flee-or-fight response to profit, how disruptions occur (performance vs. time trajectories), why incumbents ignore disruption, mini-mills vs. integrated steel mills case, automotive industry disruption (Toyota/Kia/Hyundai vs. Ford/Acura/Genesis), platform and product power, e-commerce and merchant models (Myntra), course outline and evaluation components, Microsoft FarmBeats

# Incumbents Response

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This source is a lecture session (FRNT – Session 10 by Vishal K) examining how incumbent firms respond to disruptive competitors, primarily through the strategy of platform envelopment. It uses Microsoft Teams vs. Zoom and Microsoft Teams vs. Slack as core case studies to illustrate how incumbents leverage bundling and existing customer bases to neutralize challengers. The session also covers GE's transformation from a product company to a platform company through the Industrial Internet and IoT, and introduces a typology of business models along upstream/downstream and integrative/focused dimensions.

## Key Claims

- Zoom's success was driven by user-centric design, simplicity, reliability, and strategic expansion into non-enterprise segments (educational, familial, personal communication)
- Microsoft Teams initially focused on competing with Slack in team collaboration, not on video communication or non-enterprise features, which created Zoom's opening
- Microsoft employed platform envelopment against Zoom by bundling video communication into its multi-platform offering — a tried-and-tested method previously used against WordPerfect, Lotus, and dBase via the Office Suite
- In platform envelopment, firm B enters firm A's market with a multi-platform bundle, and A is vulnerable if it cannot match the bundle's appeal
- Microsoft Teams dramatically overtook Slack in daily active users by 2020, illustrating the effectiveness of envelopment strategy by incumbents

## Topics Covered

Platform envelopment, Microsoft Teams vs. Zoom, Microsoft Teams vs. Slack, incumbent competitive strategy, Zoom's success factors, bundling as competitive weapon, GE Industrial Internet, IoT (Internet of Things) and smart components, business model typology (platform/solution/focused/integrative), product-to-platform transformation, multi-platform bundling, ecosystem vs. single-firm business models

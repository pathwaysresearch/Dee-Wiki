# Incumbents' Blind Spot: Profit Lens and Non-Adjacent Threats

# Incumbents' Blind Spot: Profit Lens and Non-Adjacent Threats

## Core Insight

The Innovator's Dilemma and non-adjacent competition are mutually reinforcing failure mechanisms. An incumbent's profit-driven lens causes it to evaluate all competitive threats through the lens of its own business model and margins — systematically undervaluing low-end or out-of-industry entrants. At the same time, because incumbents only scan familiar rivals within their own industry, they structurally miss disruptors arriving from adjacent or entirely different sectors. Together, these two dynamics compound a dangerous blind spot.

## How They Reinforce Each Other

1. **Profit-driven evaluation narrows scanning scope.** Incumbents chasing higher margins dismiss nascent threats that appear unprofitable or peripheral. This is the core of the Innovator's Dilemma: a disruptor's early performance looks irrelevant when measured against incumbent metrics.

2. **Non-adjacent origin amplifies invisibility.** When the disruptor comes from outside the industry entirely, it does not even appear on the incumbent's competitive radar. The profit lens is not applied because the threat is never detected in the first place.

3. **The combination creates a double filter.** First, incumbents do not look outside their industry. Second, even if they noticed an out-of-industry entrant, their profit-driven lens would likely cause them to dismiss it as not yet competitive. Both filters must fail simultaneously for an incumbent to respond early — which rarely happens.

## Strategic Implications

- Incumbents need threat-scanning processes that explicitly look outside industry boundaries, not just at traditional competitors.
- Evaluating disruptive threats through existing profitability metrics is a category error; disruptors require a different performance trajectory lens.
- The window for response closes as disruptors move upmarket, gradually delivering performance that meets mainstream customer needs.

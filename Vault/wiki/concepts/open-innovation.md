# Open Innovation

A model for R&D where companies leverage external collaboration and ideas to redefine their innovation process, rather than relying solely on internal efforts. A model where companies use external knowledge from experts, suppliers, and users for innovation, as internal R&D alone is no longer sufficient for growth. A process of using external communities to generate ideas and solutions, which is less effective for broad, conceptual problems and may not produce breakthrough innovations. A model for R&D that involves crowdsourcing ideas and solutions from outside an organization, often through contests and platforms.
